Frequently Asked Questions

How does the AI chord finder work?

Upload an audio file (MP3, WAV, M4A, OGG, or FLAC) and our AI analyzes the harmonic content to detect chords, key, and timing. Results include a playable chord timeline you can follow along with the audio.

What audio formats are supported?

We support MP3, WAV, M4A, OGG, FLAC, AAC, WMA, and WebM files up to 30 MB. For best results, use high-quality recordings.

Is it really free?

Yes, completely free with no signup required. No account needed, no usage limits, no hidden fees. Just upload and play.

How do I read chord notation?

Chords use standard Western music notation. Capital letters like C, G, D represent major chords. A lowercase "m" means minor (Am = A minor). Numbers indicate extended chords like G7 (dominant seventh). The chords show when to change during the song.
