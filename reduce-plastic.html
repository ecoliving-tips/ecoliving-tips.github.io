10 Simple Swaps to Reduce Plastic

Published on June 22, 2025 • 5 min read

Plastic pollution is one of the most pressing environmental issues of our time. Each year, millions of tons of plastic waste enter our oceans, harming marine life and ultimately making its way into our food chain. The good news is that small changes in our daily habits can make a significant difference. Here are 10 simple swaps to reduce your plastic consumption:

1. Reusable Water Bottle

One million plastic bottles are purchased every minute around the world, and most end up in landfills or the ocean. Investing in a stainless steel or glass water bottle eliminates the need for disposable plastic bottles and can save you money in the long run.

2. Cloth Shopping Bags

A single plastic bag can take up to 1,000 years to degrade. Keep reusable shopping bags in your car or by your door so you'll remember to bring them when shopping. Lightweight cotton or mesh produce bags are great for fruits and vegetables.

3. Bamboo Toothbrush

Over one billion plastic toothbrushes are thrown away every year in the United States alone. Bamboo toothbrushes offer a sustainable alternative with biodegradable handles and come in plastic-free packaging.

4. Beeswax or Silicone Food Wraps

Instead of plastic wrap or aluminum foil, try beeswax wraps or silicone covers. Beeswax wraps are made from cotton infused with beeswax, jojoba oil, and tree resin. They're washable, reusable, and last about a year.

5. Safety Razor

Plastic disposable razors contribute to an enormous amount of waste. A stainless steel safety razor is built to last a lifetime - you only need to replace the metal blades, which are recyclable.

6. Glass or Stainless Steel Food Containers

Plastic food containers can leach chemicals into your food, especially when heated. Glass or stainless steel containers are safer, more durable alternatives that won't absorb odors or stains.

7. Reusable Straws

Americans use 500 million straws daily. Switch to reusable options made from stainless steel, glass, silicone, or bamboo. Many come with cleaning brushes and carrying cases for on-the-go use.

8. Bar Soap and Shampoo

Liquid soaps and shampoos typically come in plastic bottles. Bar versions work just as well without the packaging waste. You can even find conditioner bars, lotion bars, and solid dish soap.

9. Cloth Napkins and Towels

Paper napkins and paper towels often come wrapped in plastic and contribute to deforestation. Cloth napkins and reusable towels are more absorbent, durable, and create less waste over time.

10. Bulk Shopping

Buying food, cleaning supplies, and personal care items in bulk reduces packaging waste. Bring your own containers to stores that offer bulk options, or choose products with minimal or recyclable packaging.

Start Small and Build

Remember, the goal isn't perfection but progress. Start with one or two swaps that seem most manageable, then gradually incorporate more as they become habit. Every small change adds up to make a significant positive impact on our planet.
