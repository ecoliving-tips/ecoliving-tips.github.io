Frequently Asked Questions

What is a chord progression?

A chord progression is a sequence of chords played in order. Common progressions like I-V-vi-IV are the foundation of countless hit songs across all genres.

What are diatonic chords?

Diatonic chords are the 7 chords naturally formed from a major or minor scale. In C major, these are C, Dm, Em, F, G, Am, and Bdim. They all sound good together.

What is the most common chord progression?

The I-V-vi-IV progression is the most popular in modern music. In the key of C, that's C-G-Am-F. It's used in thousands of pop, rock, and worship songs.

How do I use this chord progression builder?

Select a key from the dropdown, then click diatonic chords to build your progression. Use preset buttons for common progressions. Press Play to hear your progression, or Copy to share it.
